**Proposal due:** Week 12 (11/13) 
**Final critiques:** Week 16 (12/11)

## Proposal Requirements

Give a 5 minute presentation. Here's how to structure your presentation: 

**Part 1: one minute:** describe your project in a nutshell. We don't need to get too into the technical details. 

**Part 2: four minutes:** find 2-4 works of art that are related to the project, or that have helped shape your thinking. At least one of the artworks you show should include AI in some way, but they don't all have to be AI works.
## Summary

The final project is open-ended, feel free to use whatever tools, media or presentation strategy that makes sense for your idea.
## Requirements

Your work must engage with AI in some capacity at a technical and/or conceptual level. You are allowed to use whatever tools to make your work. You also do not have to use any AI tools, but in that case, your work should in some capacity be about or related to AI and its many contexts, and acknowledge it with intention and criticality.

### Wall Text

This time, your work must have a written wall text component that you distribute to the class before the critique. Your wall text must include:
1. **Title**
2. **Medium** (e.g. video, installation, performance.) (how many channels of video? Audio?)
3. **Duration** (if time-based)
4. **Dimensions** (if video, or if a print)
5. **Materials** (we will talk about how to write this appropriately when working with AI models)
6. **Necessary context for the work**. This should a short paragraph (or two) that provides necessary context to understand the piece. You don't need to tell us what the piece is about here, but you should provide information that helps us understand. For example, if you are using your own images or input to drive the AI, or if you trained your own model, make sure to mention that.
7. **Credits**: credit all of the different tools, platforms or other artists/makers
## Expectations

You are presenting this project as a finished work. Even if it is in progress, present your work to us as a finished product. You should also consider the way you're presenting the work. Is it meant to be projected onto a big screen? displayed on a CRT monitor? Viewed on a phone? Or website? If you're presenting in a non-traditional way, consider how to invite your audience into engaging with your work.

## Critique

Working with AI as an artistic tool brings up challenging questions around artistic authorship and agency. How much of this project is my own? A piece of feedback I gave about a few midterms was that in some cases, it felt maybe too much like assembling the outputs of an AI model. Although this doesn't necessarily make for "good" or "bad" art, the more it feels like the AI is responsible for the output of your work, the more we must rely on you–the artist–to defend and contextualize these outputs.
