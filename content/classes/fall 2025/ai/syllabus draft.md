### Trajectory

Ultimately, this is a studio class, so we need to come away with some practical way of approaching making with these tools. Given the breadth of the mediums and modes of making that fall under the AI umbrella, this is *not* a class about a specific tool (although we will introduce various tools)

Conceptually, it is important to me that people understand at a base level...

1. How machine learning works. As in, the concept of training a model, of "linear regression" as a concept. What is back propagation. What is gradient descent, what is linear algebra. weights and biases, etc. convolutional neural networks. transformers. attention. features, patterns in data. we need lots of examples of how these models train, from the simplest neural network to a really complex image generating model.
2. What "AI" encompasses. When we say AI today, we're talking about machine learning, really. But within that, maybe driving home what it really means to "learn from data", the different goals and outcomes, the different ways data is created and learned from...
	1. synthetic data / digital twins. Entire efforts that go into creating fully realized simulations that are photorealistic enough to train computer vision algorithms, like a robot in an amazon factory. creating synthetic humans to train face detection algorithms on an equal proportion of different phenotypes...
	2. Reinforcement learning as a way of teaching a machine to make decisions. The best way to do a task by creating narrowly defined parameters and reward conditions. Teaching an AI to play starcraft, or a car to navigate something
	3. large image data sets
	4. Generative "pretrained" Transformers (Scale AI + massive shadow labor force of people writing QA pairs)
