## Letter to Paul Octavious

https://www.instagram.com/pauloctavious/

Hi Paul,

My name is Doug Rosman, and I'm an assistant professor at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago, teaching in the Art and Technology / Sound Practices department. I'm reaching out to invite you as a visiting artist to come to one of my classes to talk to my students.

My department has a budget for a modest honorarium of $200, which I know isn't much, but I wanted to bring that up *before* the pitch.

Here's the pitch:

This fall semester (which began this week), I'm teaching a studio art course I developed called "AI", which is an extremely open-ended class built around critically engaging with AI through making. The class has about 15 students, mostly undergraduate but some graduate, and covers a broad range of tools, approaches and contexts. The curriculum is not designed around building expertise with these strange new technologies, but exploring these technologies creatively as a way to understand how they fit into our artistic practices, and how they shape and affect our lives more broadly.

I started following you on Instagram at some point in the last two years, after some of your suburban landscape "photographs" and videos went viral. I like the AI-related works you've shared (what I've seen, anyway), and your photography work as well (I love your photo series of Cricket Hill by Montrose Beach).

I'm interested in the ways your AI work integrates with your larger photographic and creative practice, and I would love to hear you tell us about that. From what I can tell, AI isn't a huge part of your practice, and that's partly why I wanted to invite you. I'm interested in how artists from all walks are navigating these things, both as creative tools, but also as this thing that we're all kind of forced to deal with all the time now.

The format of your visit is flexible. I'm imagining something like a casual artist talk, or maybe a dialogue between you and I in front of the class, followed by a Q&A / discussion with the class, or something like that. I would want this to be fun and interesting for you, so if this is something that sounds like it could be either of those things, let me know! I would also be happy to talk beforehand if you have any questions, either on Zoom or in person (you being Chicago-based is an added bonus!)

The class is on Thursdays from 9a-3p (with an hour break in the middle). I'd be interested in having you visit anywhere between mid-October to mid-November.

Looking forward to hearing from you!

Warm regards,
Doug
drosman@saic.edu

P.S., let me know if you'd like to see a copy of the syllabus. Also, you can find some of my work here: dougrosman.com and on Instagram (@thedougrosmanstory), in case you wanted to know a bit more where I'm coming from.
