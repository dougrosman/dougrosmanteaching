## Assignments

Obsession

Over the next week, let yourself become obsessed with something. An idea, an image, a word, it doesn't matter. It doesn't have to exist in the world, but it can. It can be dreamlike, or unexplainable. It can be extremely straightforward or simple. Settle on that thing, and describe it, as clearly as you can in **30-50 words.**

Then, take this written idea, and use it as a prompt with a variety of text-to-image models (I've provided a list below)
1. Use the same prompt with each model. **You must stick with the same prompt.**
2. Generate 4-8 images with the same prompt with each model
3. Compare all of your outputs, and decide which model gave you the outputs you liked the most.
4. To compare all your outputs, create an account on [Milanote](https://milanote.com/)
5. Create a board in Milanote
6. Drag your images onto your board and arrange them. (You can add up to 100 cards to Milanote with the free version)
7. Add a note card to your Milanote with your original prompt, and note cards for each of the models. 
8. Go back to the model you liked most and spend some time with your prompt. Refine it and tweak it to try to get closer to...who knows what? Let yourself be guided by intuition. Keep going until you feel like stopping (or you run out of credits).
9. Add those images to the same Milanote board

(The example I showed in class was my brief obsession with the idea of a car crashing inside of a convenience store)

Here are the platforms and models you should explore:

1. ChatGPT (https://chatgpt.com/) (Free)
2. MidJourney (https://www.midjourney.com/home) (Free)
3. OpenArt.ai (https://openart.ai) This is website platform that makes it easy to try a lot of different models all in one place. Unfortunately, you'll have to pay to use it, the free credits won't be enough to try make many images. You can pay $14 for one month of access.
4. The models you should try are:
	1. Flex Kontext Pro
	2. Nano Banana
	3. Flux (dev)
	4. Flux (pro)
	5. Seedream 3.0
	6. Qwen Image
	7. Ideogram V3
	8. Imagen 4
	9. HiDream l1
	10. Flux Krea

Many of the models above are open source and can be used in ComfyUI, but each requires downloading large model files and setting up workflows. ComfyUI may be free, but paying $14 to use OpenArt will save you a lot of time.

**Notes about this assignment (why are we doing this?)**

My intention with this assignment is twofold:

1. To practice a mode of working with AI that is intuitive, and based more on process than output.
2. To get used to iteration and moving between tools. There is value in finding a model or approach you like and sticking with it, but it's important to try a bunch of things too.
