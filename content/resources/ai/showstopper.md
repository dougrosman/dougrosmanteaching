This is a chronicle of my experiments with the new Amazon-backed personally-tailored AI-generated slop TV show maker "Showstopper." This is, at the time of writing, a Discord-based service that allows you to create your own "TV shows", where the default is some generic adult cartoon dialogue with a stiff Rick and Morty art style. You pick the characters, location, and other settings, and you can either write a scene prompt (where the service generates the dialogue for you), or you can write the script yourself.

I hate everything about this.

https://www.avclub.com/read-this-showrunner-netflix-of-ai-interviewer

The service also allows you to "cast yourself." I named my character Dou Grosman and upload a photo of myself (which the platform cartoonified) and recorded some short audio snippets to clone my voice. I took a crappy photo of my face, and then selected the body. The website's body selection tool randomly generates your body from a bank of options, and frequently glitches when toggling between bodies.  
  
I don't have a screenshot, but the body I thought I was picking was a version of the body below, but hybridized with a suit jacket. It turns out it was just glitching, and this was the actual body I was selecting. I didn't intend to pick this body for my scenes, but I'm happy with it.
